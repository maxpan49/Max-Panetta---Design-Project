The FIFA World Cup

What is the World Cup?

The World Cup is an international soccer competition hosted by FIFA, the governing body of association football, every four years since 1930 with the exception of 1942 and 1946 due to World War II. The World Cup works on qualification with 32 competing international teams, where 16 can make it and only zone country can win. The location of this amazing occurence is chosen by bids between countries. The hosts are automatically qualified into the World Cup. This event is the most loved and anticipated soccer tournament in the entire world. It is very special to the billions of fans and it's a moment to look forward to. The FIFA World Cup is also the most watched sporting event of all time. In 2006, one ninth of the entire planet was watching the spectacular event. Brazil has always been leading in having the most of these titles, currently holding five and have never failed to qualify. Italy and Germany come in second place, holding four. France and Argentina also do very good. Winning the World Cup is the best thing that can happen to a player and it is a super significant moment in their life which means everything to them. The next World Cup is set to take place this year on November 21st and will end on December 18th, one of the rare times it happens in this season. It will take place in Qatar.
